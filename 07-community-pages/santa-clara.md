## Why Santa Clara?

Santa Clara offers something increasingly rare in Silicon Valley: relative value. Home to Intel, NVIDIA, and Santa Clara University, the city provides strong employment, solid schools, and a central location — all at price points more accessible than neighboring Cupertino or Sunnyvale. For first-time buyers and families looking to enter the Silicon Valley market, Santa Clara is one of the smartest choices available.

### Key Highlights

- **Population:** ~130,000
- **Median Home Price:** ~$1.7M
- **Top Employers:** Intel, NVIDIA, Applied Materials, Palo Alto Networks, Santa Clara University
- **Commute:** Central 101/237/880 access, Caltrain (Lawrence & Santa Clara stations), VTA
- **Lifestyle:** Levi's Stadium (49ers), Santa Clara Convention Center, Great America, Mission Santa Clara, diverse dining

## Neighborhoods to Know

### Rivermark
Master-planned community with newer homes, townhomes, and condos. Walking trails, parks, and Rivermark Village shopping. Popular with young families and tech professionals.

### Pomeroy / Washington
Established neighborhood near Santa Clara University. Tree-lined streets, mid-century homes, and walkable to campus amenities. Strong community feel.

### Scott Park / Briarwood
Affordable, well-maintained neighborhoods with larger lots. Close to Central Park and Scott Lane Elementary. Great for families seeking space.

### Old Quad
Historic area near the Santa Clara Mission and University. Charming older homes, walkable to restaurants and cultural venues.

### Agnew / Montague
North Santa Clara near Levi's Stadium and the 237 corridor. Mix of single-family homes and newer townhome developments. Convenient commute location.
