## Why Palo Alto?

Palo Alto is the intellectual and cultural heart of Silicon Valley. Home to Stanford University, the venture capital corridor of Sand Hill Road, and a thriving downtown, it attracts buyers who value education, culture, and proximity to the tech ecosystem. The real estate market here reflects that prestige — it's one of the most expensive and competitive in the nation.

### Key Highlights

- **Population:** ~68,000
- **Median Home Price:** ~$3.5M+ (varies significantly by neighborhood)
- **Anchor Institution:** Stanford University
- **Commute:** Caltrain (downtown station), 101, 280; walkable/bikeable downtown
- **Lifestyle:** University Avenue dining, Stanford Shopping Center, Baylands Nature Preserve, vibrant arts and culture

## Neighborhoods to Know

### Old Palo Alto
Palo Alto's most prestigious neighborhood. Grand homes on tree-lined streets, close to downtown and Stanford. Median prices regularly exceed $5M.

### Crescent Park
Large lots, mature landscaping, and proximity to Rinconada Park and the Lucie Stern Community Center. Highly desirable for families.

### Professorville
Historic district with Victorian and Craftsman homes. Walking distance to University Avenue. One of Palo Alto's most charming neighborhoods.

### Midtown
Family-friendly neighborhood with a village feel. Midtown Shopping Center, local restaurants, and excellent schools. Strong community identity.

### Barron Park
Tucked along the foothills with larger lots and a quieter pace. Home to the famous Bol Park donkeys. Popular with families seeking more space.

### College Terrace
Small-scale neighborhood near Stanford with cottages and bungalows. Walkable to campus and California Avenue dining district.
