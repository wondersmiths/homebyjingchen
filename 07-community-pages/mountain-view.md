## Why Mountain View?

Mountain View has transformed from a quiet suburban city into one of Silicon Valley's most dynamic communities. Anchored by Google's sprawling Googleplex campus, the city offers a walkable downtown, diverse dining, excellent transit options, and a strong sense of community. It's a top choice for tech professionals who want to live close to work without sacrificing lifestyle.

## Neighborhoods to Know

### Old Mountain View
Walkable to downtown Castro Street. Charming older homes, some Eichlers, and new townhome infill. The most sought-after location for those who want urban-village living.

### Cuesta Park
Family-oriented neighborhood centered around Cuesta Park. Well-maintained homes from the 1950s-60s. Excellent access to Bubb Elementary and Graham Middle.

### Waverly Park
One of Mountain View's premier neighborhoods. Larger lots, mature trees, tree-lined streets. Highly rated Huff Elementary school. Competitive and expensive.

### Rex Manor
More affordable entry point into Mountain View. Ranch-style homes with good lot sizes. Close to San Antonio Shopping Center and 101.

### Shoreline West
Near Google campus and Shoreline Amphitheatre. Mix of single-family homes and townhomes. Convenient for Googlers and tech workers.

### Gemello
Quiet neighborhood near the Los Altos border. Larger lots, hilly terrain, and proximity to Springer Elementary. A hidden gem.
