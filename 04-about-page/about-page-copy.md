## The Story

### It Started With a Search of My Own

When I first arrived in Silicon Valley, I was the one searching for a home.

I remember the feeling — the excitement mixed with overwhelm, the endless open houses, the offers that didn't go through. I was a newcomer navigating one of the most competitive real estate markets in the world, and I felt like I was always one step behind.

I didn't have someone in my corner who truly understood what I needed — not just in terms of square footage and school districts, but in terms of what *home* meant to my family.

That experience changed everything for me. It's the reason I became a realtor, and it shapes how I serve every single client today.

---

### What I Bring to the Table

I approach real estate the way the best advisors approach any high-stakes decision: with data, preparation, and a clear strategy.

Before we ever step into a home together, I've already studied the market. I know what comparable homes have sold for. I know which neighborhoods are appreciating and why. I know the school ratings, the commute times, the hidden gems that don't show up on listing sites.

But strategy without empathy is just math. What sets my approach apart is that I pair analytical rigor with genuine care for the people I work with. I listen before I advise. I ask the questions other agents skip. And I never push a timeline that doesn't serve your best interests.

---

### Negotiation Is Where It Matters Most

In Silicon Valley, finding the right home is only half the battle. *Winning* it is the other half.

I've built my reputation on winning competitive offers — not by overpaying, but by crafting compelling offers that speak to sellers beyond just price. I understand what motivates the other side, and I use that insight to position my clients for success.

For sellers, I bring the same strategic intensity. My listings don't just go on the market — they're positioned, staged, photographed, and marketed to attract the strongest possible offers. I manage the process from preparation to close so that nothing is left to chance.

---

### More Than a Transaction

The relationships I build with my clients don't end at closing. Many of my clients become friends. They call me when they're thinking about renovating, when they're curious about the market, when their friends need an agent they can trust.

That's the part of this work that means the most to me. Real estate is one of the biggest decisions you'll ever make, and I don't take that lightly. When you work with me, you're not getting a salesperson. You're getting a partner who will be as invested in your outcome as you are.
